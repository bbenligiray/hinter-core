# Baron William's Reply re: Stonebridge

- Young Peter returned from Baron William's lands. He delivered Lord Robert's message to the Baron's seneschal, as the Baron himself was "indisposed".
- The reply is a short, formally polite note. It acknowledges our message.
- It states the activity at Stonebridge is merely "routine maintenance of existing structures" on the Baron's lands and "no cause for alarm".
- It makes no mention of our claim to lands south of the river, nor does it agree to or decline a meeting of representatives, simply stating they "value good neighbourly relations".
- Lord Robert is, predictably, angered by the dismissive tone. He sees it as a clear deflection. He is now contemplating a more direct response.
